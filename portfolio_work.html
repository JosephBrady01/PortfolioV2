Small Brief: Cleversteam search result redesign

During my University course I had to complete a Portfolio module, this module consisted of 3 tasks: create a portfolio website and complete a large & small task. I had a limited number of tasks to chose from, some from acutal companies & some were fake scenarios created to be as close to industry standard as possible.

For my small brief I chose Cleversteam's search results redesign. The project involved a search system for different volunteers for different sorts of schemes, what they wanted was a way to see more information about the candidate without having to go to another page.

My interpretaion of the brief was that Clevesteam wanted a new way to view more information whilst keeping to the basic retangular tile layout. The premise was to be able to let employers view an applicant on the search page, rather than, have 5 tabs open for different applicants. The overall goal is to give an employer the information needed to interview the applicant, saving time loading multiple pages for each applicant.

The first step in the project was to create a wireframe from the brief, I decided to make two different designs, one utilizing text and another utilizing icons to bring down the amount of text on the tile. As you can see from the above images 2 & 3.

The next step in the cycle was to turn the wireframes into prototypes using the Figma programme. Represented by images 4 & 5 I believe that the first design although the icons were a good idea taking up less text on the til overall the text orientation/order wasn't thought through and came out not knowing where one section ended or not. The second design , represented by images 6 & 7 was the design I chose for the final build, I thought that the use of colour to distinguish the short card from the expanded area was very good and that the text was aligned much better than the previous design.

The final step was to code the tile design that I chose, I believe that because I followed the cycle well the final result is very similar to the protottype design. If you would like to see how the project turned out then click the repository link below.
